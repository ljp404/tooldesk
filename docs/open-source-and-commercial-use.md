# Open Source And Commercial Use

tooldesk is released under the MIT License. See [`../LICENSE`](../LICENSE).

## What The License Allows

The MIT License permits private use, commercial use, modification, distribution and sublicensing, subject to the license text.

If you redistribute a modified version, keep the copyright and license notice required by the MIT License.

## What The License Does Not Grant

The project license covers the tooldesk source code and documentation that are owned by this project. It does not grant rights to third-party trademarks, logos, service names, APIs, datasets or other assets owned by their respective providers.

Third-party names may appear in the product or documentation only to describe compatibility, user-configured integrations or common service endpoints. They must not be used to imply endorsement, sponsorship, affiliation or official partnership.

## Trademark And Product Positioning

tooldesk is an independent desktop toolbox and plugin platform. It must not be marketed as an official client, fork, compatibility layer or endorsed replacement for any third-party product unless that relationship is formally approved.

Avoid public-facing wording such as:

- "official uTools compatible client"
- "uTools clone"
- "official Obsidian/KeePassXC/QQ Mail client"
- "endorsed by" any third-party service

Preferred wording:

- "desktop toolbox"
- "local plugin platform"
- "supports user-configured integrations with ..."
- "can open or search locally configured ..."

## Third-Party Icons And Assets

Before publishing a release, confirm every non-original icon, logo, dataset and bundled asset has a compatible license or has been replaced by an original asset.

For conservative public releases:

- Use original tooldesk icons for tool cards and taskbar icons.
- Use third-party names only as text labels when needed.
- If a third-party logo is necessary for recognition, document its ownership and usage basis in `NOTICE.md`.
- Do not include third-party screenshots or marketing images unless their license permits redistribution.

## Commercial Builds

Commercial distribution is allowed by the MIT License for tooldesk-owned code, but a commercial build still needs to:

- comply with third-party dependency licenses,
- avoid implying endorsement by integrated services,
- keep private release credentials out of the client repository,
- provide a clear privacy/data notice for features that read local files, clipboard, browser bookmarks, mail accounts or password databases.
